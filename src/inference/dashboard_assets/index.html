Problem definition

What this project asks and why it matters

The problem

The MBTA serves over 1 million riders per day and adds an estimated $11.5 billion per year to the Greater Boston economy. Yet only 31.7% of buses are on time, and the routes serving low-income and minority neighborhoods consistently underperform. We need to (a) quantify how unequal the service is and (b) build models that can predict delays accurately enough for real-time passenger information.

Two-part scope

  • Q1 - Q7 equity analysis: ridership, travel times, wait times, citywide delays, target-route gap, service disparities, demographic correlations.
  • Q8 delay prediction (extended research): six iterations of ML models on a strict temporal split (train less than 2025, test 2025+).

Data scale

  • 161 million bus arrival/departure records (2020-2026)
  • ~18 GB raw, converted to Parquet for fast I/O
  • 2,910 bus stops mapped to 22 Boston neighborhoods
  • 210 routes with demographic profiles built from Census/ACS

Headline result

From V1 (R^2 = -0.07, worse than predicting the mean) to V6 Transformer (R^2 = 0.9942, RMSE = 0.46 min) - 93% RMSE reduction across six iterations. Feature engineering (lag + FFT + wavelet) drove most of the gain; model architecture provided the final refinement.

Model progression V1 -> V6

From R^2 = -0.07 to 0.9942 across six experiments

Six iterations on a strict temporal split (train less than 2025, test 2025+). V1 and V2 fail with negative R^2, demonstrating that delay prediction is a short-term dynamics problem. V3 lag/FFT/wavelet features lift R^2 by +1.05 in a single step. V5 NeuronSpark SNN and V6 Transformer push the ceiling further at ~1.5M parameters.
